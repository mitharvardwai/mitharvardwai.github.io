Krista Pullen is a third year Biological Engineering PhD student in the Lauffenburger Lab at MIT. She utilizes machine learning to characterize the adaptive immune response to infectious diseases, focusing primarily on cross-species modeling in the context of vaccine development.

Krista graduated from Stony Brook University with a B.S. in both Biology and Applied Math, and a minor in Bioengineering. She worked as a Research Technician at Tufts University with Professor Bree Aldridge in the microbiology department for a year before entering the Biological Engineering PhD program at MIT. Her appointments include a National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship and a Biological Engineering Data Lab Fellowship.
